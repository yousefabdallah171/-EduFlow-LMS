# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Arabic-speaking students in Egypt and the MENA region, ranging from mobile-first learners on phones to desktop learners. Primary language is Arabic (RTL); English (LTR) is a secondary option. Users access the platform on mobile, tablet, and desktop — all three viewports are equally important. The single admin is the course instructor (Yousef Abdallah), who manages lessons, students, payments, and media from desktop.

## Product Purpose

A private, instructor-branded single-course LMS. Students register, pay via Paymob, and watch AES-128 encrypted HLS video lessons with a personal watermark. The platform is branded under the instructor's name (Yousef Abdallah), not a generic marketplace. Success means: students can enroll and learn without friction; the instructor retains 100% revenue and full control.

## Brand Personality

Focused, trustworthy, professional. The interface earns confidence through clarity and restraint — not through decoration or visual noise. The brand speaks to adult learners who want results, not a consumer entertainment product.

## Anti-references

- AI agent dashboards and hacker/terminal aesthetics (dark neon, matrix green, glowing borders)
- Generic SaaS marketplaces (Udemy's cluttered catalog feel, badge-heavy layouts)
- Gamification excess (streaks, confetti, leaderboards that distract from learning)
- Corporate sterility (pure grey enterprise software with no warmth)

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — every element serves a function. Ornamentation that doesn't aid comprehension is noise.
2. **Trust through consistency** — the instructor's brand must feel stable across every surface: payment flow, video player, admin, profile.
3. **RTL-native, not RTL-retrofitted** — Arabic is the primary language. Every layout decision should hold in RTL without visible compromise.
4. **Mobile-first, desktop-refined** — the majority of students arrive on phones. Design for the small screen first, then enhance for tablet and desktop.
5. **Error states are first-class** — payments fail, videos buffer, uploads stall. A hardened interface treats these paths as designed experiences, not afterthoughts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Full bidirectional support (RTL Arabic, LTR English) across mobile, tablet, and desktop. Logical CSS properties throughout — no hardcoded `left`/`right`. Test at 200% zoom. Sufficient color contrast in both light and dark themes.
